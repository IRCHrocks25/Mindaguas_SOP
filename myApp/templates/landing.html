The Complete Framework

Everything You Need to Evaluate
the Right AI Pilot.

From opportunity identification to enterprise-wide scaling — StrategicAI Advisors gives operations leaders a structured, repeatable methodology that eliminates guesswork and vendor-driven decisions.

Phase 1
Opportunity Identification
"Find the pain points worth solving — before you touch a vendor."

Structured gemba walks and interviews across all warehouse functions. Document use cases with measurable problem statements, affected KPIs, annual cost estimates, and data availability assessments.

01
Phase 2
Prioritization & Selection
"Score and rank use cases — not by novelty, but by operational impact."

A weighted scoring matrix evaluates each opportunity across operational impact, data readiness, implementation complexity, strategic alignment, time to value, and vendor maturity. Feasibility validation follows before any commitment.

02
Phase 3
Pilot Design & Planning
"Define success before you deploy — or you won't know if you've won."

Draft a formal Pilot Charter with objective, hypothesis, scope, 60–90 day timeline, and RACI. Quantitative and qualitative success criteria — from picks per hour to operator adoption rate — must be documented with baselines before go-live.

03
Phase 4
Pilot Execution
"Shadow Mode, Guided Mode, then Assisted — never go full-auto on Day 1."

A phased deployment model: AI runs in shadow mode first, then operators follow its recommendations with override capability, then AI drives decisions under human oversight. Weekly structured reviews track KPIs, operator feedback, and integration stability throughout.

04
Phase 5
Evaluation & Go/No-Go Decision
"Four outcomes: Scale, Iterate, Pivot, or Stop — all data-driven."

A rigorous evaluation report covers pre/post KPI comparison, ROI calculation, operator adoption, technical reliability, and risk assessment. A structured decision matrix guides the executive sponsor to a defensible scaling or stop decision at the final gate.

05
Phase 6
Scaling Roadmap
"Translate a successful pilot into a structured enterprise deployment."

Facility prioritization, resource planning, phased rollout timelines, capital and OpEx forecasts, and a cross-functional AI Center of Excellence. Continuous improvement cycles ensure ongoing ROI and model performance don't degrade post-launch.

06
Governance
Roles, RACI & Change Management
"AI pilots fail from people problems, not technology problems."

Defined roles from Executive Sponsor to Frontline Operator — with named accountability at every step. Pre-launch communications, hands-on training, visible leadership presence in the pilot zone, and a dedicated associate feedback channel are non-negotiable.

07
Vendor Strategy
Vendor Evaluation Framework
"Structure paid POCs with milestone payments and exit clauses — always."

Evaluate vendors on solution fit, warehouse domain expertise, integration capability, data transparency, implementation support, commercial flexibility, and security compliance. Two reference calls with comparable warehouses are mandatory before any contract is signed.

08
Risk Management
Six Pitfalls Operations Leaders Must Avoid
"The six most common ways warehouse AI pilots fail — and how to prevent each one."

Boiling the ocean. Skipping baselines. Vendor-driven scope. Ignoring the frontline. Vanity metrics. No exit criteria. Each pitfall is documented with root cause and a concrete prevention strategy inside the SOP.

The Six-Phase Process

From Pain Point to Proven ROI
in a Single Pilot Cycle.

A repeatable end-to-end lifecycle that warehouse operations leaders can run internally — without external consultants holding your hand at every step.

1
Audit Your Operations

Conduct structured interviews and gemba walks. Catalog every pain point with a measurable problem statement and estimated annual cost.

2
Score & Select

Run the weighted scoring matrix across all use cases. Validate feasibility — data, infrastructure, workforce readiness — before committing.

3
Design the Pilot

Write the Pilot Charter. Set your hypothesis, success criteria, baseline KPIs, and Go/No-Go thresholds — all before a single vendor is engaged.

4
Execute in Phases

Deploy in Shadow, then Guided, then Assisted mode. Weekly reviews track KPIs, operator feedback, and model performance against baseline.

5
Evaluate & Decide

Present the results report to the Executive Sponsor. Scale, iterate, pivot, or stop — based on pre-defined criteria, not gut feel or vendor pressure.

6
Scale Across Facilities

Prioritize sites by readiness and expected impact. Stand up the AI Center of Excellence and establish continuous improvement cycles for sustained ROI.
